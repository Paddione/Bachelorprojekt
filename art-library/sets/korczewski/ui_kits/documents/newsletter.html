Issue 014 — Patch notes · v1.30

Quietly faster, and a little stranger.

Three weeks of work, four breaking changes you can probably ignore, and one new command you won't be able to live without. Here is what landed in v1.30.

By K. Korczewski · 11 min read
§01Scheduling, redone §02Runbook diff view §03Breaking changes
§01

Scheduling, redone.

We rewrote the scheduler to read like English. Predicates are now first-class objects, and the explainer no longer lies about preferred-but-not-required.

Added

Predicate composition

Combine predicates with any(), all(), and not(). The scheduler explainer prints the boolean tree on a single line of mono.

— K.K. · #4421
Added

Topology spread budgets

Set a percentage budget per zone instead of a hard count. The scheduler will redistribute on cordon without re-evaluating preferences.

Fixed

Preempt loop on tainted nodes

A pod that landed on a freshly-tainted node could trigger a preempt of itself within the same scheduling cycle. Closes #3892.

The scheduler explainer is the first kubectl command I run during an incident now. It tells me why, not just what.
— Maya Oduya, Head of Platform · Tradesman
§02

Runbook diff view.

Three lines of code, three weeks of design. The runbook now shows you what changed since the last incident — including who edited each step and why.

§03

Breaking changes.

Breaking

The --legacy-explain flag is gone

It was deprecated in v1.27 and produced output nobody parsed correctly. The new explainer is the only path.

Deprecated

Imperative kore taint

Use the declarative taint manifest. We will remove the imperative form in v1.32.

Upgrade — paste this in your runbook 
$ kore upgrade --to 1.30 --dry-run

✓ 3 charts compatible

$ kore upgrade --to 1.30 --confirm
Until next month — keep the pager quiet.
